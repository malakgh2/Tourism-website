Chak Wak Park Museum in Tozeur This incongruous, trippy park is a testament to the vision of the former mayor of Tozeur. Scattered around gorgeous grounds is what amounts to a 3D education on evolution, history and religion. Highlights include life-sized replicas of dinosaurs, a gallery of icons of the world's major religions and a good history of Hannibal and the Carthaginian wars. It's all tremendous fun, and there's a particularly well-situated restaurant, pizza place and cafe.

Aboul Qacem Echebbi

monument


Aboul-Qacem Echebbi (Arabic: أبو القاسم الشابي‎, ALA-LC: Abū al-Qāsim al-Shābbī; 24 February 1909 – 9 October 1934) was a Tunisian poet. He is probably best known for writing the final two verses of the current National Anthem of Tunisia, Humat al-Hima (Defenders of the Homeland), which was originally written by the Egyptian poet Mustafa Sadik el-Rafii..
